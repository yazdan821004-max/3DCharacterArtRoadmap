◈ Practice Exercises — When & What
Wk 1–2
Daily Maya interface drills. Model 5 simple hard-surface props from reference photos (mug, box, chair, lamp, tool).
Wk 3–4
UV unwrap your 5 props in Maya. Practice clean UV layouts — this is muscle memory you'll use forever.
Wk 5–6
First ZBrush sessions. Follow Pavlovich's YouTube series. Sculpt 10 simple organic forms (rocks, bark, cloth folds).
Wk 7–10
Copy anatomy plates from Zarins — draw/trace them. Do 2–3 pages per session. Do this with pencil and paper.
Wk 11–16
Sculpt a basic human mannequin in ZBrush from a sphere — proportions only, no details. Repeat 3 times until proportions feel natural.
✦ Stage 1 Milestones — Check Before Moving On
Can navigate Maya comfortably and model clean hard-surface props
Can UV unwrap a mesh without major distortion
Can perform core ZBrush operations: Dynamesh, masking, subtools, layers, transpose
Can name and locate the major muscle groups of the body from memory
First ZBrush mannequin figure completed with believable proportions

ANATOMY IN ZBRUSH

Sculpting the Human Form

⏱ Estimated: 4–5 Months

This is where theory meets clay. You will apply everything you've read in Zarins' books into actual ZBrush sculpts. Do not rush this stage. It is the foundation your entire career will stand on.

▲ Courses
Artistic Anatomy
Dan Crossland
ZBrush anatomy · Structured muscle-by-muscle approach
Male Anatomy & Female Anatomy
Raf Grassetti · Gumroad
5+ hrs male · Blocking → secondary → texturing · Separate female extension
Character Facial Sculpting
CGMA
Head planes · Facial anatomy · Expressions in ZBrush
◈ Practice Exercises — When & What
Wk 1–4
Sculpt an écorché figure (muscle by muscle, from skeleton out). Use Zarins Book 1 as your guide. Don't rush — spend 1 week per body region.
Wk 5–8
Male torso study — 3 full passes: block, secondary forms, surface detail. Do not move on until each pass feels solved.
Wk 9–12
Female anatomy study using the same 3-pass method. Pay attention to differences in fat distribution and pelvis width.
Wk 13–16
Sculpt 5 different head studies from photo reference. Each one should have a different age, ethnicity, and expression.
Wk 17–20
Full-body male figure with detailed face, hands, and feet. This is your first full anatomy character. No costume.
✦ Stage 1.5 Milestones
Can sculpt a full male anatomy figure with correct muscle landmarks
Can sculpt a believable female form with correct proportions
Understands head planes and can sculpt secondary facial anatomy
Has a personal study library of 10+ ZBrush anatomy sculpts
First full-body anatomy figure complete and presentable
Can name any muscle from reference while sculpting it

CHARACTER PIPELINE

Full Character Creation

⏱ Estimated: 5–6 Months

Now you sculpt with purpose. You will learn the full pipeline: high-poly sculpt → retopology → UVs → baking → first portfolio piece. The Ackeem Durrant course alone is 47+ hours — budget your time accordingly.

▲ Courses
Creating a Character Bust
Gnomon Workshop
High-poly character bust · Sculpting, detailing, render
Character Creation for Games
Ackeem Durrant · Vertex School
47h 18m · Full AAA game character pipeline · PBR workflow
⊕ Added to Your Plan
Retopology deep practice: After each major sculpt, do a full manual retopology pass in Maya. ZRemesher alone won't get you hired.
Marmoset Toolbag: Learn basic setup for baking and final portfolio renders. This is how AAA artists present their work on ArtStation.
UV Mastery: Spend a dedicated week practicing UDIM UV workflows — essential for realistic characters.
First ArtStation post: Post your character bust publicly before this stage ends. Feedback is the fastest path to growth.
◈ Practice Exercises
Wk 1–4
Create a stylized character bust from a concept art you love. Focus on translating 2D to 3D — proportions, silhouette, personality.
Wk 5–8
Retopology bootcamp: take 3 different high-poly meshes and do clean hand-retopo. Target game-ready poly counts.
Wk 9–12
Follow the full Ackeem Durrant course with a character of your own choosing (not the instructor's concept).
Wk 13–16
UV unwrap & bake your Ackeem-style character. Set up your first Marmoset scene with proper lighting and materials.
Wk 17–24
Personal portfolio character — fully independent. Concept selection, sculpt, retopo, UVs, bake, render. This goes on ArtStation.
✦ Stage 2 Milestones
First portfolio-quality character bust published on ArtStation
Full pipeline: high poly → retopo → UV → bake → presentation
Can hit game-ready poly count targets without losing visual quality
Confident with UDIM UV workflows and Marmoset Toolbag
2–3 ArtStation posts with process breakdowns written

05
NEW · PORTFOLIO & INDUSTRY

Get the Job

⏱ Estimated: 2–3 Months

This stage didn't exist in your original plan — and it's the one that converts skill into employment. No amount of skill matters if your portfolio isn't curated, presented, and in front of the right people.

▲ Actions
Curate ruthlessly: Select only your 5–8 absolute best pieces. Remove anything you'd be embarrassed to show at an interview.
Write process posts: For each piece, write a full ArtStation breakdown with wireframes, textures, and process images.
Study the job listing: Read Naughty Dog and similar studios' character artist job postings. Make sure every requirement is in your portfolio.
Network actively: Follow and interact with character artists on ArtStation, Twitter/X, and LinkedIn. Be a real person in the community.
Apply wide, aim high: Apply to junior roles at multiple studios. A junior role at any AAA studio is the path to Naughty Dog.
Never stop learning: Even while applying, do one new study per week to keep your skills sharp and show recent activity.
✦ Final Milestones — You Are Ready
ArtStation portfolio is curated and professional
Every major AAA pipeline skill is represented
Can complete a full character independently from brief to render
Industry artists have given positive feedback on your work
Applications submitted to 10+ studios
First interview or art test received
